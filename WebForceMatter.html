What are States of Matter?

Explore States of Matter Simulation

States of matter are forms in which matter exists.

The four states of matter observed in everyday life are solids, liquids, and gases.

States of Matter

States of Matter

Solid

A solid is a state of matter with a defined shape and volume.

Liquid

A liquid is a state of matter with a defined volume, but no defined shape.

Gas

A gas is a state of matter lacking either a defined volume or defined shape.

What is Force?

Forces and Motion

A force is a push or pull that can change the way something moves.

A force can make an object start moving, stop moving, or change direction!

Types of Forces

Types of Forces

Contact Force

Contact forces represent the result of physical contact between two objects where one object exerts force on the other.

Non-Contact Force

Non-contact forces do not involve physical contact between two objects but act through the space between the two.

Examples of Contact Forces

Contact Forces

Friction

Friction is a force that resists the sliding or rolling of one solid object over another.

Tension

Tension is a force transmitted through a rope, string, cable or similar object when pulled by forces acting from opposite sides.

Spring Force

Spring force is the restoring force exerted by a spring when it is either stretched or compressed from its equilibrium position.

Examples of Non-contact Forces

Non-contact Forces

Gravity

Gravity is the mutual attraction between all masses in the universe.

Electric Force

Electric force is the repulsive or attractive interaction between any two charged bodies.

Magnetic Force

Magnetic force is the attraction or repulsion between electrically charged particles due to their motion.

What is Motion?

Motion

Motion happens when an object moves from one place to another.
